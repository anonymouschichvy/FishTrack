Acoustic Sounding Explainer

How Echo Sounding Transmissions Work

What does this monitor do? It parses and displays ultrasonic sonar returns. The buoy transducer emits sound pulses into the water and listens for echoes that bounce back. When a sound wave encounters an object of different density (like a fish's air-filled swim bladder or the muddy seabed), it creates an echo. The time delay measures distance, while echo strength indicates target size.

Why is it important? Sonar works continuously in turbid, dark, or muddy water where cameras cannot capture visual images, making it essential for tracking fish school movements overnight.

Sonar Monitor Docs

Transducer Frequency: The buoy utilizes a 200 kHz frequency transducer. High frequency (200 kHz) provides excellent resolution for small targets (like individual shrimp or bait fish) in shallow coastal waters (under 100 meters).

Sonar Troubleshooting

No fish detected? If the detection rate remains 0% for an extended period, verify:

  • Transducer is fully submerged in the water.
  • No biofouling (barnacles or algae) on the transducer face.
  • Buoy is not listing/tilted due to heavy waves (which causes the sonar beam to point sideways).

Glossary

Ultrasonic Transducer
A device that converts electrical energy into high-frequency sound waves and vice versa.
Biomass Target
A generic term representing organic matter detected by acoustic waves, typically representing individual fish or schools.
