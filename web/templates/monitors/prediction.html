About Fish Prediction

How are predictions calculated? The prediction engine uses seasonal availability registers from the authoritative BFAR MIMAROPA dataset (compiled from Marinduque Provincial Fishery Office records in SPECIES COMMODITY.docx) and correlates them with real-time buoy telemetry. The presence probability is determined by combining seasonal windows, GPS geographic proximity, camera YOLO optical detections, and sonar acoustic biomass echoes.

AI-Powered Predictive Insights

Google Gemini AI Integration: Detections from the computer vision model (YOLOv8) are first verified against the BFAR database. The Gemini API analyzes family-level taxonomical connections (such as elevating related species under the Tuna, Scad, or Mackerel families if one is captured) and evaluates environmental match factors. A 3-paragraph biological narrative is generated in real-time to summarize the buoy's telemetry and prediction indicators.

Onboard Telemetry Metrics

Camera YOLO Detections
The onboard camera runs a real-time object detection model (YOLOv8) to capture optical frames of fish. These scientific classifications are verified against BFAR taxonomy using the Gemini API to identify connected family species.
Sonar Acoustic Biomass
A 200 kHz echo sounder scans the water column to count underwater acoustic targets. Schooling pelagic species correlate with high echoes and sonar sweep densities.
GPS Spatial Positioning
Coordinates from the active GPS receiver are matched against municipal distribution zones (e.g. offshore deep trenches or shallow coastal shelves) preferred by each species.
BMS & Battery Health
LiFePO4 cell voltage, charge percentage, and charging states monitored by the BMS. Low battery levels trigger warnings and switch the buoy to power-saving schedules.
