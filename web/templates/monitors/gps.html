How Marine GPS Drift Tracking Works

What does this monitor do? It maps the current coordinates and historical tracking paths of buoy nodes in the field. Each buoy queries its onboard GPS module to calculate its location using timing signals received from global navigation satellites.

Why is it important? Ocean currents and winds cause buoys to drift. Continuous geographic tracking prevents device loss and provides valuable drift rate data representing local tidal and marine currents.

GPS Tracking Docs

Fix Quality Levels: Fix quality represents the spatial accuracy of coordinates based on the sat lock method:

  • 3D Fix: Locked to 4+ satellites, providing lat, lon, and altitude. High accuracy (under 5m).
  • 2D Fix: Locked to 3 satellites, providing lat and lon only. Medium accuracy.
  • No Fix: Insufficient satellites. Search mode active.

Anchor Geofencing

Drifting Alert: Buoy settings allow configuring a geofence radius. If a buoy's coordinates exceed the fence radius, a warning notification is displayed in the notification drawer.

Glossary

GNSS
Global Navigation Satellite System, the umbrella term representing satellite navigation networks like GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, and BeiDou.
NMEA Sentence
Standard data format sent by GPS receivers containing coordinates, speed, and time metrics.
