Power Systems Explainer

Understanding Marine Node Energy Harvesting

What is this monitor? This interface displays live telemetry from the intelligent Battery Management System (BMS) integrated into each buoy node. It monitors the 4-cell Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4) pack, tracking current draw, harvesting efficiency, and individual cell balance.

Why is it important? Marine nodes rely entirely on solar power and battery storage. If a cell drops below its critical discharge threshold, the BMS triggers a low-voltage cutoff to protect chemistry health, causing the node to go offline. Early warning allows base station operators to retrieve or troubleshoot struggling buoys.

Battery Health Docs

LiFePO4 Chemistry: Lithium Iron Phosphate is safer and lasts significantly longer than traditional LiPo chemistry. They are ideal for high-vibration, high-temperature marine deployments.

Troubleshooting

Cell Unbalance: If any single cell deviates by more than 150 mV from the others, the BMS will prioritize balancing. If the deviation persists, cell health may be degraded.

Glossary

BMS (Battery Management System)
Electronic controller protecting cells from overvoltage, undervoltage, and overcurrent conditions.
VBUS
Voltage at the input of the charging bus (e.g. from the solar harvesting panel).
C-Rate
The rate of discharge or charge relative to the battery's nominal capacity.
